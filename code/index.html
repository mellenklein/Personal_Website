< about >

I am a Front End Engineer in Nashville, TN, learning to code at The Iron Yard in downtown Nashville. I will graduate in January 2016.

Originally from Atlanta, my love for using languages began at a young age. Creative writing and poetry sparked my interest first, and then I discovered my passion for foreign language.

I studied Japanese starting in fourth grade, and twelve years later I earned my Bachelors degree in Japanese Language and Literature.

Meanwhile, my innate passion for writing poetry led me to become a songwriter and musician during high school and college, which eventually led me to Nashville in 2011 to pursue music full time.

In late 2013, while editing my band's website, I became frustrated by the limitations on customizing the design. One thing led to another, and I discovered my great passion for learning the languages of the web. I began teaching myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. As my curiosity grew more insatiable, I knew it was time to fully immerse myself in the programming world. I enrolled in The Iron Yard's Front-End Development program in 2015 and will graduate in January 2016.
